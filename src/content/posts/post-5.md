After arriving at the Cayce Riverwalk for the first time, I couldn't help but notice the boat entrance, accompanied by an unused dock. It only peered into the lake, but it also allowed me to see the greenery facing the water from the lake, along with the backdrop of the sun shining on it. And it was here that I would stake out for 2 hours.

![image](/photos/blog-5/snake.png)

While the plan was to photograph birds, the first creature I found was none other than a brown water snake on the dock. He was none too happy to see me; he eventually slunk through the dock and back onto the water.

![image](/photos/blog-5/wren.JPG)
The birds were quite elusive in this waterfront park. The first one to reveal itself was the South Carolina state bird: the Carolina Wren. It jumped down into the clearing before immediately flying to another tree. 


![image](/photos/blog-5/cardinal.png)

The cardinals, though, were in abundance. As seems to be the case with cardinals, they were very evasive. I came across many of them through this trip. A pair of cardinals was flying together through the trees before hopping along the ground. I suspect one was attempting to court the other. I am still looking for a truly good cardinal photo, as they still manage to elude me.

![image](/photos/blog-5/water-bird.png)

The spotted sandpiper. This was a new bird for me, and it was the only one I spotted. I suspect this waterbird is abundant if you have the patience to wait for them. I would love to get a shot of it among some foliage, so that it stands out more.

![image](/photos/blog-5/werbler-1.png)

The Carolina Chickadee—these were my "grand prize" for the day. There was a group of four that hopped from branch to branch, taking small bites where they could but ultimately playing among themselves. They constantly moved between trees, making them very difficult to capture. I only managed a few shots when one of them rested. They are easily my favorite shots of the day.

![image](/photos/blog-5/werbler-2.png)


![image](/photos/blog-5/crow.png)
Given how common grackles are, it's amazing that this was the first time I managed to photograph one. This wasn't even on the waterfront; it came from a tennis court a few miles down the road—which, in hindsight, makes sense. I'm used to seeing grackles in places where they can rummage through human trash. However, it looks like they foraged that nut all on their own.

![image](/photos/blog-5/nomo.png)

And finally: a northern mockingbird. Frankly, I'm tired of seeing these birds—they are everywhere. But at least this one let me capture an excellent shot up close. They are much more brown than I realized. I wish I had the opportunity to photograph other birds as closely.
